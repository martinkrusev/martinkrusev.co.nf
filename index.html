About Me

My name is Martin Krusev, and I am a graphic designer and web developer. My experience with many content creation tools make me proficient in many of the aspects of content creation and editing. My background in web development, graphic design and DTP for some of the most famous companies in the world gave me the inside experience to a great approach to the latest trends in content creation.
I am fueled by my passion to create and develop, and always continue to expand my professional skills. I always try to improve my knowledge of the software I work with, and working in the Desktop Publishing sphere, I am constantly challenged every day with new projects that vary not only technically, but also in creatively. My strive to keep improving myself is contributed by the projects I keep working on, some of which are from some of the biggest companies in the world. As a DTP Specialist, I work with many famous brands such as AMD, General Mills, Aramark, 3M, S&P Global Platts, Nike, UPS and others.
Meanwhile, while I work on those projects, I get an incredible inside view of their design processes. I always try to learn more and improve my skillset each time. Sometimes working as DTP requires me to even create content, by special client requests.
My dedication to my work, and the strive to perfection in each job helped the companies I worked for win over some major clients. With the help of my coworkers, we developed trust between our company and our clients, always looking for quality and on-time delivery. Even when problems arose with some projects, my ability to work in cooperation with big production teams, meant that the client was always left satisfied with the job we have done.

Opticentre

Desktop Publishing Specialist • May 2016 - August 2017

Opticentre is a professional localization, translation and multilingual desktop publishing (DTP) service provider that was established in 2006 in Bulgaria and the US. I worked for the company as a DTP specialist and a web designer. Some of my responsibilities included Desktop Publishing, Web Development and Quality Assurance. During my stay, I learned new and expanded many of my skills in Adobe CC, Microsoft Office, HTML, CSS, XML and others.
My main responsibility was to work closely with other coworkers in the Production team, to format, localize and typeset documents in languages many consider exotic. My dedication to my work was quickly noticed, and my ability to problem solve and learn quickly gave me the recognition of my colleagues and superiors. Soon into my training period I was given more and more responsibilities, and was soon looked after by coworkers who had far more experience in the area than me. I was soon after asked to take the position of Project manager. My humility told me I should stay in my current position as I still had more to learn as a DTP. Soon after I was head of the newly created Web Development team, where I was tasked with creating and maintaining websites for clients. Until my leave from the company, I was given the opportunity to supervise and do final checks of the projects that the company was creating, before they were handed to the end client.
